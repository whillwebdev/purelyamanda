[yoga]

I discovered yoga while attending college. I quickly realized it was the most powerful tool for me to manage my stress and learn to take better care of my body following a miserable decade of extreme dieting and obsessive gym visits. I fell in love with yoga immediately. For a while, it was more of a privilege or treat; dropping in to local studios when I had some extra cash or wherever donation classes were offered. When I arrived on contract to spend a year in South Korea, I spent a lot of my downtime focused on educating myself further and practicing almost daily.

During this time, I knew deep in my bones that I wanted to teach yoga on some sort of level. As fate would have it, I had the unique opportunity of inheriting a weekend beach yoga class an instructor was giving up as she was ending her contract and leaving the country. Each week the class grew as expats from all over the world joined me every Saturday morning on Gwangali Beach, Busan, South Korea. As the class size grew, so did my confidence. And as my contract ended, I knew my next stop was yoga teacher training. I chose Yoga Tree (now Yoga Works) in San Francisco and in the matter of one incredible month, earned my certification and followed my first teaching gig back in Tampa, FL.

I've been in the Tampa Bay Area ever since, growing, evolving and becoming the best yoga instructor I yearn to be. I never know what opportunity may pop up next, but one thing I know for sure is that I have finally found my true calling. You can catch me on schedule at various studios teaching all styles of yoga including vinyasa flow, yin, restorative, hatha and guided meditation. I also recently introduced my new specialized workshop entitled "YogArtiste: When Art, Music and Yoga Collide". I am also available for private instruction both individual and small groups.
